Top 8 FAQ's

Elephants are wild animals that have been taken from the wild and gone through severe training and domination to allow people to ride them. In most places in Nepal, they are fed an unnatural limited diet, and they live in small dirty shelters, by themselves. The elephants are not allowed to behave naturally chained and controlled throughout their lives to make a profit for their owners with elephant rides and baths. The total weight of tourists (around 300 kgs) and placement of the howdah (a tool for elephant safari passengers to sit and hold) on the elephants’ back for riding does damage and injuries to an elephant’s back with the number of rides and amount of time elephants are made to work every day. Several elephants in Nepal have scars on their back from the howdah. Elephants are dangerous animals. Every year domestic and international tourists and mahouts are injured and sometimes killed by captive elephants.
